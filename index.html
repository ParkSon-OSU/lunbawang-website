About the Lubawang

The Lubawang people are one of the indigenous ethnic groups of Sarawak, mainly found in the Lawas and Limbang districts. They have a rich cultural heritage, unique traditions, and a strong sense of community rooted in the highlands of northern Borneo.

Traditions & Customs

The Lubawang community is known for their colorful traditional attire, communal celebrations, and unique rituals that mark life events such as marriages and harvest festivals.

Food & Lifestyle

Their cuisine often features locally sourced ingredients cooked in bamboo or over open fire. Popular dishes include pansoh (bamboo-cooked rice and meat) and various smoked or fermented foods.

Music & Dance

Music and dance play important roles in ceremonies. Traditional instruments like gongs and bamboo flutes accompany dances performed during harvest and community celebrations.
